Until now, the Generative AI we have experienced has been more of a brilliant "conversationalist." It answered questions and summarized text when asked. However, the tech industry's focus is rapidly shifting beyond models that merely speak well toward "AI Agents" that can autonomously complete given goals.

An AI agent is an autonomous software system that handles complex, multi-step tasks with minimal user intervention. It moves past simply generating text to perceiving its environment, reasoning to create a plan, and directly calling the necessary tools to execute that plan.

### From the Era of Questions to the Era of Execution

Early chatbots remained passive interfaces, providing immediate answers to individual user prompts. However, in the business world, there has been a significant demand for automating complex workflows rather than just receiving isolated answers. This created a need for autonomous execution models that can interact with various external systems, self-correct errors during tasks, and reach a final objective.

Against this backdrop, AI agents possess a unique cycle of Perception, Planning, and Action.

- **Autonomous Decision-Making:** They determine the optimal path to achieve a goal without humans dictating every single step.
- **Perception and Planning:** They gather external data to understand the context, and based on this, a Large Language Model (LLM) breaks the task into smaller, manageable steps.
- **Tool Utilization:** They perform actual work through APIs or dedicated software. This enables tangible "actions," such as sending emails or deploying code.

### The Decisive Difference Between Chatbots and Agents

While many confuse chatbots with agents, there is a clear boundary between the two concepts. If a chatbot is an interface that provides information through "communication" with a user, an agent is an "action-oriented" system that effects direct changes in the environment outside the system.

For example, when asked for travel plans, a chatbot simply recommends places to visit and a potential schedule. In contrast, an AI agent takes the user's budget and preferences, books the flights, reserves the accommodation, and finalizes the process by registering the itinerary in the user's calendar.

### The Rise of Agentic AI Transforming the Workplace

This agent technology is already proving its effectiveness in various industrial fields. A prime example is the "Coding Agent." When a developer provides general requirements, the agent reviews existing code, identifies vulnerabilities, generates modified code, and handles deployment and validation in a real test environment.

Furthermore, discussions regarding "Multi-Agent Systems"—where multiple agents collaborate—are gaining momentum. In this structure, agents with different specializations divide complex projects among themselves, moving like a team. In this process, "Tool Use"—the ability of an agent to access external databases or software to execute functions—has become the core technical mechanism determining the success of the system.

### From Tools to Partners

The proliferation of AI agents will fundamentally change how we interact with software. Users will no longer have to worry about "How" to do something; instead, they will focus on "What" they want to achieve. AI is evolving from a simple assistant into a partner with the power of execution.

The eyes of tech companies worldwide are now fixed on how far this new layer of technology, which thinks and acts autonomously, can push the efficiency of business processes.
