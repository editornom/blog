# What is MTTR?

## Definition
MTTR is an abbreviation for 'Mean Time To Repair' or 'Mean Time To Recovery.' It represents the average time elapsed from the moment a system or equipment failure occurs until it is restored to its normal operating state. In IT Service Management (ITSM) and infrastructure operations, it serves as a Key Performance Indicator (KPI) to measure the maintainability and availability of a system.

## Practical Use Case
In environments where Agentic AIOps is implemented, AI agents analyze telemetry data to autonomously identify the root cause of failures. These agents can then execute recovery tasks, such as server reboots or service restarts, without human intervention. This automated process significantly reduces the time required for manual intervention by operations teams, thereby shortening the MTTR and enhancing overall service availability.
