Until recently, generative AI has largely remained a "responsive interface," simply answering user questions or generating sentences. However, the latest technological shift is moving beyond mere text generation toward "AI Agents"—autonomous intelligence systems that can judge, act, and produce tangible results. Once a high-level goal is set, an AI agent independently designs the necessary sub-tasks, calls the appropriate tools, and completes the work.

**Autonomy Created by the Fusion of Reasoning and Tools**

The core distinction between an AI agent and a traditional chatbot or assistant lies in its capacity for autonomous planning and execution. While traditional systems were limited to assistive roles—searching for and summarizing information based on specific user prompts—an agent explores the necessary paths itself as long as the final destination (the Goal) is clear.

This mechanism operates on a cyclical structure often summarized as "Think-Act-Observe." Using a Large Language Model (LLM) as its brain, the agent analyzes the current situation and formulates a plan (Think). It then performs commands by directly interacting with external software through web browsing, database access, or API integration (Act). Finally, it enters a feedback loop (Observe), reviewing whether the results align with the goal and adjusting its strategy accordingly to improve the quality of the outcome.

**An Autonomous Execution Entity Beyond a Simple Assistant**

While existing AI assistants guide users on "how to perform Task A" or filter information for them, an AI agent aims to deliver a report stating "Task A has been completed."

For example, when planning a trip, an assistant might list flight prices and hotel information, leaving the final selection and payment to the user. In contrast, an AI agent calculates the optimal route based on the user's budget and preferences, then directly accesses flight booking APIs and payment systems to finalize the actual reservation. This represents a transition from the stage of Cognition to the stage of Action.

**Changes in the Field and Technical Synergy**

From a practical business perspective, the application of AI agents is opening new horizons for process automation. In a customer service scenario, an agent can understand the context of an incoming complaint, retrieve relevant regulations from an internal knowledge base (RAG), and then log into an Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) system to directly process a refund or exchange. This process requires minimal human intervention and, thanks to its ability to process unstructured data, can flexibly handle complex exceptions that traditional RPA (Robotic Process Automation) could not solve.

- **Agentic AI:** The technological trend where AI operates as an active agent rather than a static tool.
- **Tool Use:** Organic interaction with external software such as web search, calendars, and payment systems.
- **Reasoning Engine:** The decomposition of multi-step tasks based on the logical thinking capabilities of an LLM.

Ultimately, AI agents use the powerful reasoning of an LLM as their "brain" and various API interfaces as their "limbs" to secure autonomy within the digital environment. This shift goes beyond automating simple repetitive tasks; it serves as the driving force behind creating a truly intelligent work environment where humans can focus solely on high-level decision-making.
